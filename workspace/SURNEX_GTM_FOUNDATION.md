# Surnex GTM Foundation

_Date: 2026-03-16_

## Brutal Starting Point

Surnex is not in growth mode yet.
Surnex is in **proof mode**.

Current reality:
- MVP exists
- no customers
- no MRR
- first customers must be agencies
- pricing direction is credit-based but not finalized
- current assets are limited: website wireframe on `surnex.io` and an app

That means the immediate marketing job is not "scale demand."
It is:
1. define the sharpest agency wedge
2. validate that agencies will pay for it
3. get first logos
4. extract proof and language from those wins
5. only then widen distribution

---

## Category Read

Surnex sits across three overlapping categories:

1. **Traditional SEO platforms**
   - rank tracking
   - backlinks
   - site audits
   - keyword research

2. **AI visibility / GEO / LLM brand monitoring tools**
   - prompt tracking
   - AI answer visibility
   - citations / mentions
   - competitive gaps

3. **Agent-first workflow / automation layer**
   - work executed by agents or APIs rather than only dashboards

This is both an opportunity and a risk.

### Opportunity
Most competitors are strong in only one or two layers.
If Surnex genuinely unifies them, that matters.

### Risk
If the story is too broad, buyers will hear:
"another AI SEO platform that does a bit of everything."
That is death by vagueness.

## Competitor Map

## 1) Direct / emerging AI search visibility competitors

### Peec AI
Positioning observed:
- "AI Search Analytics for Marketing Teams"
- focuses on AI search metrics like visibility, position, sentiment
- emphasizes prompts, citations, reports, and AI model tracking

Takeaway:
- strong direct competitor on AI visibility analytics
- likely easier for market to understand because the promise is narrow
- weaker than Surnex if Surnex can credibly combine this with broader SEO + workflows

### Waikay
Positioning observed:
- AI search optimization and prompt tracking
- emphasizes brand understanding, hallucinations, missing knowledge, competitor gaps, citations
- explicitly targets agencies and CMOs
- already uses credit-based packaging language in pricing

Takeaway:
- this is an important competitor signal because it validates credit-based pricing in the category
- also a warning: if we use the same model without better packaging, we’ll look derivative
- Waikay leans into reputation / AI narrative control; Surnex should avoid sounding like a clone

### Rankscale
Observed claim:
- "Track and Deeply Analyze Visibility in AI Search Engines"

Takeaway:
- another category-validation player
- likely narrower than Surnex
- useful as proof that AI search tracking is a real, growing budget line

### Profound
Could not fetch cleanly in this environment, but it is a known player in AI answer / AI search visibility discussions.

Takeaway:
- should be treated as part of the real competitor set for deeper future research
- especially relevant if they are moving upstream into enterprise brand intelligence

## 2) Large indirect competitors expanding into AI visibility

### Semrush
Observed positioning:
- now explicitly talking about brand visibility across digital channels
- includes AI visibility index, LLM prompts, AI visibility toolkit content, enterprise AIO language

Takeaway:
- Semrush is no longer just a legacy SEO suite; it is actively colonizing AI visibility territory
- enterprise buyers may default to Semrush if they can get "good enough" AI visibility there
- Surnex should not try to beat Semrush by breadth right now; it must beat them on clarity, speed, agency workflow, and modern category focus

### Ahrefs
Observed positioning:
- now calls itself an AI marketing platform powered by big data
- explicitly mentions brand & AI search, citations, sentiment, reporting, API access, chatbot integrations

Takeaway:
- same problem as Semrush: they are moving into the narrative
- buyers already trust them
- if Surnex sounds like a smaller all-purpose suite, we lose
- if Surnex is sharper for agencies and more useful in the AI-search transition, we have a lane

## Strategic Conclusion on Competitors

The real competitive battle is not against one company.
It is against three buyer alternatives:

1. **use Semrush/Ahrefs and wait**
2. **buy a narrow AI visibility tool**
3. **do manual analysis with smart people + AI + spreadsheets**

Surnex must beat those alternatives with a message like:

> Agencies need one modern search operating layer that covers AI visibility and core SEO, without paying for bloated enterprise complexity or stitching together five tools.

## First Customer ICP

### Primary ICP: small to mid-size agencies

Best first target profile:
- 5 to 40 people
- SEO / growth / content / performance-led agencies
- manages multiple client accounts
- already does recurring reporting
- feels pressure to talk about AI search / AI Overviews / ChatGPT visibility
- does not want to hire more strategists just to keep up

### Why this ICP first
- pain is real and immediate
- buyer is close to delivery pain
- sales cycle is shorter than enterprise
- they need reporting leverage
- they care about new service packaging and differentiation

### Avoid first
- giant enterprise agencies with long procurement cycles
- tiny freelancers with no budget and chaotic workflows
- in-house teams before agency wedge is proven

## Core Wedge

The wedge should not be "all SEO and AI search for everyone."
That is too fat.

The wedge should be something like:

> Surnex helps agencies turn AI search visibility into a sellable, reportable client service — without losing the SEO data they already rely on.

That is stronger because it speaks to:
- revenue expansion for agencies
- service delivery efficiency
- client reporting clarity
- transition from classic SEO to modern search

## Website Wireframe Review (`surnex.io`)

## What is working
- headline direction is broadly correct: AI visibility + SEO together
- agency audience is present
- feature set matches market direction
- CTA to start free is good if the product is actually usable

## What is weak

### 1. It is still too generic
The site says many true things, but not enough sharp things.
A lot of the copy could be swapped with another AI SEO startup and nobody would notice.

### 2. Audience focus is muddy
Parts of the page speak to agencies, but other parts broaden to developers, SEO teams, marketers, and AI products.
At this stage that is a mistake.
We need one clear hero audience first.

### 3. Proof is thin
"Trusted by modern agencies" and testimonial-style quotes are dangerous if the company is this early.
If they are placeholders, fine internally — but weak or synthetic proof on a real site can burn trust fast.

### 4. Product breadth may be hurting clarity
The page lists lots of capabilities. Good for later. Not good for first understanding.
The buyer needs to know in 5 seconds:
- who it is for
- what painful job it does
- why this is different now

### 5. Agent-first advantage is underexploited
The current site mentions agent readiness, but it does not yet land the punch.
If agent-first is real, we need to show why that matters operationally, not just as a buzz phrase.

## Site direction recommendation
For now, bias the site heavily toward agencies and use the homepage to sell one core idea:

> Turn AI search visibility into a measurable, reportable agency service — without adding another disconnected tool.

## Pricing Direction

Credit-based pricing can work here.
But credit systems often become conversion killers if buyers cannot predict usage.

### Rules for pricing
1. make credits map to obvious units of value
2. avoid looking like mobile-game coins for marketers
3. make entry plan simple enough to buy without a sales call
4. make agency margin obvious

### Likely better framing than raw credits alone
Use a hybrid structure:
- base plan by usage tier / tracked clients / tracked prompts / analysis volume
- credits as flexible overage or premium action currency

That will probably sell better than pure abstract credits.

## Fastest Path to First 10 Customers

## Phase 1 — sharpen the weapon
Deliverables:
- final ICP definition
- competitor comparison matrix
- homepage messaging rewrite
- simple offer + pricing hypothesis
- outreach narrative for agency founders / heads of SEO

## Phase 2 — customer discovery with intent
Goal:
- talk to agencies fast
- not generic research calls; real qualification and problem validation

Questions to validate:
- do they already feel pressure from clients around AI search?
- how are they measuring AI visibility now?
- what reporting gap is most painful?
- what would make this worth paying for in month one?
- what would make them distrust it?

## Phase 3 — land design partners / pilot customers
Target:
- 5 to 10 agencies

Offer shape:
- early access / founding partner program
- white-glove setup
- direct feedback loop
- discounted or fixed early pricing in exchange for engagement, case study rights, and speed

## Phase 4 — extract proof
Need:
- before/after reporting examples
- saved strategist time
- new service packaging examples
- client-report screenshots
- testimonial proof from real operators
